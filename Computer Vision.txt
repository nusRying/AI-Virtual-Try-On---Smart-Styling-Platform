Core Computer Vision Pipeline

Your system is essentially:

“Understand the human body → understand clothing → realistically merge them”

🧩 1. Human Pose Estimation
🔍 What it does:

Detects body keypoints:

Shoulders
Arms
Torso
Hips
🧠 Method:
Deep CNN / Transformer-based keypoint detection
📌 Models:
OpenPose
MediaPipe Pose
🎯 Why it’s critical:
Aligns clothing with body
Prevents weird placements
🧪 Example Output:
Left Shoulder: (x, y)
Right Shoulder: (x, y)
🧩 2. Human Parsing (Segmentation)
🔍 What it does:

Splits the image into regions:

Face
Hair
Shirt
Pants
Background
🧠 Method:
Semantic segmentation (pixel-wise classification)
📌 Models:
U-Net
DeepLabV3
Graphonomy (fashion-specific)
🎯 Why it’s critical:
Removes original clothes
Keeps body intact
🧩 3. Clothing Segmentation
🔍 What it does:

Extracts the clothing item (shirt, pants) from its image

🧠 Method:
Instance segmentation
📌 Models:
Mask R-CNN
U²-Net (lightweight)
🎯 Why:
Clean overlay onto body
Removes background noise
🧩 4. Geometric Warping (VERY IMPORTANT)
🔥 This is the “magic alignment step”
🔍 What it does:

Transforms clothing shape to fit body pose

🧠 Methods:
👉 Thin Plate Spline (TPS)
Smooth non-linear warping
👉 Spatial Transformer Networks (STN)
Learnable transformation
🎯 Why:

Without this:
👉 Shirt will look pasted, not worn

🧩 5. Image Generation / Synthesis (CORE)
🔥 This is where realism comes from
🧠 Methods:
Old Approach:
GANs (VITON, CP-VTON)
Modern Approach (YOU should use):
Diffusion Models
📌 Models:
Stable Diffusion-based try-on
TryOnDiffusion
🎯 What it does:
Blends clothing + body
Adds:
Wrinkles
Shadows
Lighting consistency
🧩 6. Texture & Lighting Consistency
🔍 What it does:

Ensures:

Shirt matches lighting
No sharp edges
🧠 Methods:
Neural rendering
Feature blending in latent space
🧩 7. Body Shape Understanding (Optional for PoC)
🔍 What it does:

Understands:

Slim / athletic / broad
🧠 Methods:
CNN-based body classification
Depth estimation (advanced)
🎯 Use:
Better recommendations
🧩 8. Color & Style Analysis (For Recommendations)
🔍 What it does:
Detect shirt color
Match with pants/shoes
🧠 Methods:
Simple:
Color histograms
Advanced:
CLIP embeddings (image-text understanding)
🎯 Example:

“Blue shirt → black jeans”

🔄 Full Pipeline (Put Together)
Input Image
   ↓
Pose Estimation
   ↓
Human Segmentation
   ↓
Clothing Segmentation
   ↓
Geometric Warping (TPS)
   ↓
Diffusion Model (Synthesis)
   ↓
Final Try-On Image
